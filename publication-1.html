I’m a Filipino-American designer who has lovingly called the PNW on this side of the Pacific Ocean my second home. Shaped by coastlines—Manila Bay, theSeattle Waterfront, and the Willamette River—my work follows the ebb and flowbetween architecture, history, and public life, and how these forces shape belonging. Grounded in intersectional feminist theory and human-centered design, I approach architecture with identity and lived experience as central to our understanding of the built environment. I’m especially drawn to civic and urban projects, wherearchitecture has the potential to support equity, care, and community at scale. I am fascinated by the intersection of urban design, public-interest architecture, andstorytelling—design informed by communities as experts of their lived environments.
